Bacterial infections are caused by microorganisms that enter the body through breaks in the skin. See here for Bacterial Infections resources.

Viral infections are caused by submicroscopic "agents" that infect the body and its different cells with foreign genetic material. See here for Viral Infections resources.

Dermal fungal infections are caused by fungi that attach hair follicles, nails, and the outer skin layer. See here for Fungal Infections resources.

Dermal parasitic infections are caused by parasites that burrow into the upper layers of the skin and in hair. See here for Parasitic Infections resources.

Dermal allergy, otherwise known as contact dermatitis, occurs when the skin comes in contact with an allergen. See here for Allergic Reactions resources.
